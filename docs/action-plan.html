Dalton's Action Plan

The simple version. Do these things in order and you'll get more jobs.

Your Secret Weapons (Things You Already Beat the Competition On)

Before anything else, know what you've got going for you:

  • You're open when they're closed. Fence OKC (the biggest local competitor) closes at 4PM and doesn't work weekends. You're available 6AM–10PM and weekends. That's huge.
  • You're closer to your customers. You're in Blanchard. Most competitors are in OKC. People like hiring local.
  • You build decks too. Most fence companies only do fences. You can offer more to each customer.
  • 10+ years experience. That matters to homeowners.

Step 2: Post on Facebook Every Time You Finish a Job

Take a before photo and an after photo of every single job. Even small repairs. People love seeing the difference.
Post the photos on your Facebook page with a short caption. Something like:
"New cedar privacy fence in Blanchard! 🔨 Another happy customer. Need a fence or deck? Call or text us for a free estimate: (405) 276-9765"
Share your posts in local Facebook groups. Search for: "Blanchard Community," "Norman Buy/Sell/Trade," "Moore Oklahoma Community," "OKC Home Improvement." Join them and share your work.
Sign up for Nextdoor (free). Go to business.nextdoor.com. This is where your neighbors look for contractors. Claim your business and respond when people ask about fencing.
DO THIS WEEK — $150

Step 3: Yard Signs at Every Job

Order 20–30 yard signs. Put your name, phone number, and "Free Estimates" on them. Order from VistaPrint or a local sign shop. About $5–10 each.
Ask every customer: "Can I leave a sign in your yard for a couple weeks?" Most people say yes. Put it near the road where people driving by can see it.
Why this works: Neighbors see your sign every day. When they need a fence, you're the first name they think of. Each sign brings 1–3 calls per month for basically no cost.
DO WITHIN 2 WEEKS — FREE

Step 4: Sign Up for Lead Apps

These are apps where people who already want a fence go to find a contractor. They come to you — you don't have to chase them.

Sign up for Thumbtack. Go to thumbtack.com/pros. Create a profile. Set your service area. Set a weekly budget (start with $50–100/week). People who need fences will find you.
Sign up for Angi (used to be Angie's List + HomeAdvisor). Go to angi.com/pro. Same idea — homeowners post jobs, they match you.
Sign up for Google Local Service Ads. Go to ads.google.com/local-services-ads. This puts you at the very top of Google when someone searches "fence installer near me." You only pay when someone actually calls you.

What to expect

If you spend $500/month total across these apps:

  • You'll get about 10–15 leads per month
  • If you close 30% of them on a $3,000 average job
  • That's 3–5 new jobs = $9,000–$15,000 in revenue
  • From a $500 spend. That's a great deal.
DO WITHIN 2 WEEKS — FREE

Step 5: Start a Referral Program

Tell every customer: "If you send someone our way who books a job, I'll give you $50." Give the new customer $50 off too. Everybody wins.
Text your past customers about it. Something like:
"Hey [name]! Quick question — know anyone who needs a fence or deck? If you send them my way and they book, I'll send you $50 as a thank you. Just have them mention your name. —Dalton, 4 Feathers"
Why this works: Referred customers trust you before they even meet you. They close at 3–5x the rate of cold leads. And you only pay the $50 when you actually get the job.

This matters more than anything else on this page.

When a lead comes in, do this:

  1. Call them right away. Drop what you're doing if you can.
  2. If they don't answer, text them immediately:
"Hi [name], this is Dalton from 4 Feathers Fencing. I just tried calling about your fence project. I'd love to help — text or call me back anytime. (405) 276-9765"
  1. Try calling again in 1 hour.
  2. Send one more text the next day if you haven't heard back.
  3. Try one last call on day 3. After that, move on.
The average contractor takes 42 hours to respond. If you respond in 5 minutes, you win almost every time. This alone will get you more jobs than any ad you could buy.
MONTH 2 — ~$1,800

Step 6: Send Postcards to Every House in Your Area

The post office has a program called EDDM (Every Door Direct Mail). You pick the neighborhoods, and they deliver your postcard to every single mailbox. No address list needed.

Design an oversized postcard (bigger = better response). Put a before/after photo, your phone number, "$200 off" offer, and a QR code to your website. Hire someone on Fiverr for $50–$100 or use Canva.
Print 5,000 postcards. Use VistaPrint or GotPrint. About $400 for 5,000.
Go to eddm.usps.com and pick your routes. Start with Blanchard, Newcastle, Tuttle, and south Norman. You can filter by income and household size to target homeowners.
Bundle your postcards and drop them at the post office. Postage is about $0.25 per card. For 5,000 cards, that's about $1,235.

What to expect from 5,000 postcards (~$1,800 total)

  • 50–150 people will call or visit your website
  • 25–75 of those will want a quote
  • If you close 30% at $3,000 average: 8–23 jobs = $24,000–$69,000
  • That's a great return on $1,800
Best times to send: Early spring (March–April) and early fall (September–October). That's when people think about fences.
ONGOING — FREE

Step 7: Door Hangers Near Your Jobs

Every time you're working on a job, hang 50 door hangers on nearby houses. The hanger should say: "Your neighbor just got a new fence from us! Want a free estimate?" Print them for about $0.10–$0.25 each.
Why this works: Neighbors already see your truck and crew. They're curious. A door hanger turns that curiosity into a phone call.

❌ Don't Waste Time on These

  • Don't cold call random people. Only 1–3% will even talk to you, and it can get you in legal trouble (Do Not Call laws). Let people come to you.
  • Don't blast emails to strangers. Less than 1% will respond. It's basically spam.
  • Don't pay for a fancy website redesign right now. Reviews and response time matter 10x more than a pretty website.

Instead: Let the apps (Thumbtack, Angi, Google) bring leads to you. Let postcards and Facebook bring calls to you. Then answer fast and you'll win the job.
